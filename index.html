Animal: Tiger
The tiger, a symbol of strength and majesty, roams the dense forests and grasslands of Asia. Known for its striking orange coat with black stripes, the tiger is a solitary predator, relying on its stealth and power to hunt. With a roar that can be heard up to two miles away, this magnificent creature plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of its ecosystem.

Project Moose
Animal: Moose
The moose, the largest member of the deer family, is a towering presence in the forests of North America and northern Europe. With its impressive antlers and long legs, the moose is well-adapted to its environment, navigating through deep snow and dense underbrush. Despite its size, the moose is a gentle herbivore, feeding on aquatic plants and tree bark.

Animal: elephant
Elephants, the gentle giants of the animal kingdom, are known for their intelligence, memory, and strong social bonds. Found in Africa and Asia, these majestic creatures use their trunks for a variety of tasks, from drinking water to communicating with each other. Elephants play a vital role in their habitats, helping to shape the landscape and disperse seeds.

Animal: jaguar
The jaguar, a powerful and elusive predator, prowls the rainforests and wetlands of Central and South America. With its muscular build and distinctive spotted coat, the jaguar is an expert hunter, capable of taking down prey much larger than itself. This solitary cat is also an excellent swimmer, often seen near rivers and lakes.
